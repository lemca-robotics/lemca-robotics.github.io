point-to-disk

A velocity-controlled point-mass with state \({\mathbf{x}}\) (cartesian position) must be driven into a target disk of radius \(r^* = 0.01\) m at the origin and held there. Given a velocity command \({\mathbf{u}}\), the system evolves as \(\dot{{\mathbf{x}}} = {\mathbf{v}}\) with \({\mathbf{v}}\sim B_\epsilon({\mathbf{u}}) := \{ {\mathbf{v}}: ||{\mathbf{v}}- {\mathbf{u}}||_2 \leq {\epsilon}||{\mathbf{u}}||_2 \}\) (\({\epsilon}= 0.25\) in our experiments). The actuation noise grows with the commanded speed, so slowing down (\({\mathbf{u}}\to \mathbf{0}\)) allows the controller to suppress actuation noise. The controller, operating at 50 Hz, is scored by its dwell time inside the target for rollouts of length \(H = 500\) steps (10 s) from states on the unit circle: \[\mathrm{J}(\pi) = \mathbb{E}_{\pi,\,\{{\mathbf{x}}_0 : ||{\mathbf{x}}_0||_2 = 1\}}\left[ \sum_{t=0}^H {\mathbb{1}_{\left[||{\mathbf{x}}_t||_2 < r^*\right]}} \right]\]

Fixing a coordinate system XY, the robot can move at most 1 m/s in either direction, so the action space is \({\mathcal{A}}= [-1, 1]^2\). Figure 1 illustrates a quiver plot of an oracle policy that has access to the perfect state \({\mathbf{x}}\). The policy commands the maximum possible velocity towards the target when outside the disk, and starts slowing down to zero as it enters the target disk. The oracle policy achieves a performance of \(\mathrm{J}_\text{oracle} = 456.23 \pm 4.59\) (\(N=1000\)).

Sensors The designer can equip the various controller modes with the following sensor types. Each sensor reveals a different slice of the robot’s position \({\mathbf{x}}\), as illustrated in Figure 2 .

  • Cartesian (GPS): noisy position \({\mathbf{x}}+ {\mathcal{N}}(0, \sigma_\text{xy}^2 {\mathbf{I}}_2)\), with \(\sigma_\text{xy} \in \{0,\,10^{-2},\,5{\cdot}10^{-2},\,10^{-1},\,5{\cdot}10^{-1}\}\).

  • Radial & Sector (RadialSector): a discretized polar reading returning the active sector among \(s \in [1,360]\) equiangular sectors (optionally offset by \(\phi\) radians) and the active radial band among \(\text{len}({\mathbf{d}}){+}1\) bands defined by increasing distance thresholds \({\mathbf{d}}= (d_1, \dots) \le 1\).

    • The per-step output is a concatenation of the one-hot over radial bands (empty when \({\mathbf{d}}= ()\)) and unit-vector \((\cos\theta_s,\sin\theta_s)\) corresponding to centroid of the active sector.
    • There is some Gaussian noise in measuring the polar coordinates of the robot \((r, \theta)\).
      • The noise scales are tunable with \(\sigma_\theta \in \{0,\,10^{-1},\,2{\cdot}10^{-1},\,4{\cdot}10^{-1},\,8{\cdot}10^{-1}\}\) and \(\sigma_r \in \{0,\,10^{-2},\,5{\cdot}10^{-2},\,10^{-1}\}\).

The designer’s goal is to meet a performance target \(\mathrm{J}_\text{target}\) while minimizing the sensing cost incurred.
